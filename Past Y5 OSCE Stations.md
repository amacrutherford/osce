## Year 5 June 2025

| Station | Station title | Details |
|---:|---|---|
| 1 | Neuro – Speech and language exam | Asked to assess speech and language and ‘assess the relevant cranial nerves’ in a patient – was a normal actor with no signs though. Had to give differentials for causes of dysphasia at the end. |
| 2 | Paeds – non-blanching rash (ITP) | Worried parent giving a history of non-blanching rash in their child, asked if it was meningitis. Everything in history was normal. Given blood results at the end (showed isolated low platelets) and asked to give differentials (most likely ITP). |
| 3 | GP – Fatigue history | Straightforward history of woman with fatigue, bloating and weight loss. Had to give differentials and further investigations (cannot miss cancer). |
| 4 | WRH/ORTEM – PPH A-E | Straightforward PPH A-E. Asked at the end what the 4 Ts of PPH causes are and how to manage them. |
| 5 | Psych – Alcohol/anxiety history | Bit of a weird history – patient seen in A&E with an injured wrist yesterday and asked to come in again today, you have to discuss their alcohol use/anxiety with them. |
| 6 | ORTEM – Ankle history + image/XR explanation + management | Had to take history of ankle fracture, then explain what you saw in a picture of an ankle (mainly pixelated bruises) and then explain the XR to the patient. Had to explain management (Weber A fracture – boot and weight bearing). |
| 7 | Psych – Lithium counselling | Had to talk to a worried parent about his (adult) daughter who started lithium recently. Lots of reassurance and comms. |
| 8 | WRH – Emergency contraception counselling | History and counselling for a woman who recently had unprotected sex and wanted a morning-after pill. I think ulipristal acetate was the correct option. Asked at the end about the mechanism of action of ulipristal acetate and rules for contraception beforehand (i.e. no progestogen 7d before or 5d after). |
| 9 | Paeds – Angry parent | Parent of a child with croup who was angry that she had not been told anything. You have to tell her the child needs to be moved to paeds ICU. Likely mostly comms. Asked at the end ‘Who else do you want to speak to?’ – odd question, probably the consultant and handover to paeds ICU?? |
| 10 | Neuro – Headache and loss of vision | Bit of a weird history for a woman who got an occipital headache and lost vision 2 days ago. Went to Specsavers and got a visual field test – you then had to interpret the visual field results – showed homonymous hemianopia. Asked to give differentials and management at the end – likely posterior stroke. Needs urgent neuroimaging. |
| 11 | GP – CKD and lifestyle history + management | Given table of results in instructions. Had most GP conditions you can think of – BP controlled with drugs, BMI 30, new raised urea/creatinine. Had to explain to patient they had CKD stage 3 and propose management. Some time for lifestyle suggestions e.g. smoking cessation, weight loss. |
| 12 | ORTEM – A-E Insulin overdose | Told in instructions that patient has overdosed on their insulin. Started A-E, patient initially confused. Then patient quickly lost consciousness and you had to do a proper A-E with management. Glucose dose was nicely given in a sheet of Toxbase guidelines. |
