# OSCE Exam Content Plan
# Organised by Oxford University Medical School year (6-year programme)
# Pre-clinical: Years 1–3 (BA Medical Sciences). Clinical: Years 4–6 (BM BCh).
# ✓ = already built

---

## Years 1–3 — Pre-clinical (BA Medical Sciences)

Anatomy, physiology, biochemistry, pharmacology, and pathology taught at Oxford.
Clinical skills teaching begins in Year 2 with structured patient contact and
communication skills, but formal OSCE-style examinations are limited.
No standalone examination-station OSCE at most pre-clinical assessments.

---

## Year 4 — First Clinical Year

Introductory clinical block followed by rotations in general medicine, general surgery,
and primary care at Oxford's teaching hospitals (John Radcliffe, Churchill, Horton).

### Cardiovascular
- Cardiovascular Examination
- Peripheral Vascular Examination (lower limb arterial)
- Peripheral Vascular Examination (venous / varicose veins)

### Respiratory
- Respiratory Examination

### Abdominal / GI
- Abdominal Examination
- Hernia Examination

### Musculoskeletal
- GALS Screen (Gait, Arms, Legs, Spine)

---

## Year 5 — Specialty Rotations

Rotations through orthopaedics, neurology, endocrine, O&G, paediatrics,
psychiatry, ENT, ophthalmology, and urology.

### Musculoskeletal / Orthopaedics
- ✓ Shoulder Examination
- ✓ Elbow Examination
- ✓ Wrist & Hand Examination
- ✓ Spine Examination (Cervical & Lumbar)
- ✓ Hip Examination
- ✓ Knee Examination
- ✓ Ankle & Foot Examination

### Neurology
- ✓ Upper Limb Neurological Examination
- Lower Limb Neurological Examination
- Cranial Nerve Examination
- Cerebellar Examination
- Parkinson's Disease Examination

### Endocrine
- Thyroid Examination
- Diabetic Foot Examination

### Neck & Lymphatics
- Neck Lump Examination (lymph nodes, thyroid, salivary glands)

### Surgery
- Breast Examination
- Scrotal Examination
- Rectal Examination (PR)
- Stoma Assessment
- Arterial Ulcer vs. Venous Ulcer Assessment

### Obstetrics & Gynaecology
- Gynaecological History Taking
- Antenatal Examination (symphysis-fundal height, lie, presentation)
- Speculum Examination (examination only, not smear technique)

### Urology
- Prostate Examination (DRE)
- Male Genitalia Examination

### ENT
- Ear Examination (Otoscopy, Rinne & Weber)
- Nose & Throat Examination

### Ophthalmology
- Eye Examination (Visual acuity, fields, fundoscopy, pupillary reactions)
- Red Eye Assessment

### Psychiatry
- Mental State Examination (MSE)
- Cognitive Assessment (MMSE / MoCA)
- Alcohol and Substance History
- Risk Assessment (Self-harm / Suicidality)

### Paediatrics
- Developmental Assessment (milestones by age)
- Paediatric Cardiovascular Examination
- Paediatric Respiratory Examination
- Paediatric Abdominal Examination

---

## Year 6 — Finals (BM BCh)

Finals OSCE, elective, Special Study Components (SSCs), and FY1 preparation.
Oxford Finals OSCE tests acute and emergency medicine alongside communication
and prescribing competencies.

### Acute & Emergency
- Sepsis Assessment (NEWS2, A-E approach)
- Chest Pain Assessment (ACS, PE, aortic dissection)
- Acute Abdomen Assessment
- Head Injury Assessment (GCS, NICE CT criteria)
- Shocked Patient Assessment

### Advanced Neurology
- Headache History & Assessment (red flags, differentials)
- Stroke / TIA Assessment (FAST, NIHSS, ROSIER)
- Epilepsy History Taking

### Communication Skills
- Breaking Bad News
- Obtaining Informed Consent
- Explaining a Diagnosis (e.g. diabetes, hypertension)
- Explaining an Investigation (e.g. endoscopy, CT scan)
- Capacity Assessment
- Dealing with an Angry Patient
- Telephone Consultation / Advice

### Prescribing & Clinical Reasoning
- Drug Chart Review
- Medication Reconciliation
- Interpreting Blood Results / Requesting Investigations
- ECG Interpretation
- Chest X-ray Interpretation
- Abdominal X-ray Interpretation

---

## Notes
- History taking stations are not included above but pair with most examination topics
- Oxford Finals OSCE format: typically 16-18 stations, 8 minutes each, covering all Year 4-6 content
- Communication skills stations are examined throughout Years 4-6 but weighted heavily in Finals
- Procedural skills (cannulation, catheterisation, ABG) are a separate OSCE domain and could form their own section
- Some content listed in Year 5 may shift depending on rotation order in a given cohort
